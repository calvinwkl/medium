I have an idea and want to make an app. How do I start?

Many people had their app ideas in their mind, but yet to execute. Some might lack directions of how to actually get things started. Some might just don’t know if they can get things started. This story is a list of questions and answers to this scenario.

Q: I am not a programmer, nor I had any experience to coding. Can I still make apps on my own? (iOS/Android)

A: You can. Everyone can. Of course provided that you have basic logic thinking and want to code. You often see people working in the IT industry, but their education backgrounds are not necessarily from related subjects. You have to get used to the terms and jargons we often say and the tools that we often used at the start. However, these can all be overcame by keep using, keep thinking and keep exploring. It all comes down to your dedication.

Q: I understand and I will learn to code. How do I start?

A: Go find some coding tutorial online for beginners. After you have some basic understandings of any programming language, you will be good to read any online materials for starter and my future stories. In this story, I will not write any code.

Q: How do I start Android programming? What is needed to get started?

A: You have to use either Java or Kotlin to code native android app. There are many ways to make android or ios app. The most basic approach is using the official languages, Java or Kotlin. We call it native android programming. Nowadays it is more prefered to use the latter, Kotlin.

Java or Kotlin : Native Android Programming Language
IDE : Integrated Development Environment (tools to write code)
Android Studio : Official IDE for Android programming

The IDE you will use is Android Studio. You can run on both Windows and Mac. Go download it and get the feel and click around.

Q: How do I start iOS programming?

A: You have to use either Objective-C or Swift to code native iOS app. Although you can do both, I strongly recommended you to start iOS programming with Swift. It is easier for newbie as well as veteran. Swift is open-source, easier to understand, preferred by Apple and more and more that not to be listed here. Objective-C still has its reason of existence. But as a newbie iOS developer, we do not need to care much about that.

open-source : when we say a thing is open sourced, it means its source code is open to public, constantly maintained by developers from worldwide and hence trustworthy and with quality
Q: Do I need to get a Mac or iPhone for iOS development?

A: A Mac is inevitably needed. iMac, Macbook Air or Mac mini or whatever Mac you like, a Mac is needed. You might see some teach online that you can install MacOS in your Windows, or anything that can make your Xcode work without a Mac, trust me, it is just not worth your time to do it. It requires tedious work and time. You better put those effort into your ideas and coding.

Xcode : Official IDE for iOS programming (iPhone, iPad and Apple Watch)
iOS Simulator : A virtual device for you to run in your Mac (run by Xcode)

Luckily, an iPhone/iPad is not necessarily needed. You can develop most of features with your iOS Simulators. If you do not ever need to touch these remaining features, you do not need an iPhone for development!

Real device needed features: Push Notifications, Camera, SMS, Mail etc.

Q: Do I need to pay for publishing my app to App Store or Google Play?

A: Yes. Google Play is a life-time fee $25. App Store is $100 per year.

Reminder:

  • both prices are subject to change and in USD
  • You DO NOT need to pay for coding and development.
  • You can pay the fee until you are going to launch the app, say near production stage
Q: Any programming books do you recommend?

I do not have any preference. I am sure that you can find a lot materials from Google without buying a book. Technology advances faster than you can think of. Buy eBooks, so that you can receive updates regularly, and also be environmentally friendly.
